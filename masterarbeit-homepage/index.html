Introduction

Microservice architectures enable software systems to be divided into small, independently deployable modules that collaboratively address specific business challenges. Integration within these architectures can follow either orchestration or choreography approaches. Choreography-based architectures are particularly well-suited for modern cloud environments, offering advantages such as independent deployability, loose coupling, and enhanced scalability. They rely on event-driven communication and eventual consistency without a central controller, allowing systems to better manage traffic spikes and maintain functionality even if individual services fail. Additionally, this approach supports agile development by allowing teams to work concurrently and independently on different services, resulting in flexible and resilient products without single points of failure.

Java remains one of the most widely used programming languages, making it highly relevant for microservice architectures. Its popularity and extensive tool support make it a preferred choice for many organizations. Recently, alternative Java frameworks and runtimes like Quarkus, Micronaut, and Spring Boot have emerged, specifically designed to meet the demands of cloud-based microservices with improvements in performance optimization, resource utilization, and startup speed.

A key focus of this work is the comparison between JVM-based and native Java service implementations using GraalVM. GraalVM’s Ahead-Of-Time (AOT) compilation enables faster startup times and reduced memory consumption, which are essential for distributed and cloud-native systems.

This study evaluates which of the three leading Java frameworks—Quarkus, Micronaut, and Spring Boot—performs best in a choreography-based architecture under realistic conditions concerning startup time, performance, and resource usage. By examining necessary adjustments and existing limitations, the research aims to provide valuable insights for developers and architects. These insights will assist in making informed decisions for implementing cost-effective and high-performance cloud and serverless solutions, demonstrated through a digital goods e-shop application.
